Lissajous Mantras

Visualizing the phonetic mathematics of Sanskrit mantras.

How it works

This tool generates a single continuous parametric shape per mantra for visualization. The shape is derived from Sanskrit phonetics so that each utterance has a stable mathematical signature.

Phonetic Encoding

  • Sthāna (Place of Articulation): Maps to the axis of the shape. Velar sounds (like 'a') are vertical, Labial sounds (like 'u', 'm') are horizontal.
  • Ābhyantara Prayatna (Effort): Maps to how much the base line opens into an ellipse or circle. Stops are closed (lines), Vowels are open (circles).
  • Energy: Maps to the overall scale. Silence collapses the shape to a point.

Example: oṃ (A–U–M)

  • A: Velar vowel. Pure vertical line that opens into a tall-thin ellipse.
  • U: Labial vowel. Morphs into a perfect circle.
  • M: Labial stop/nasal. Collapses into a wide-thin ellipse and ends as a pure horizontal line.
